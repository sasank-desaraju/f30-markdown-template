What my PI said in teams yesterday (5/16):


I think the overall structure is strong, especially the emphasis on antigen escape and aim independence, but the draft still needs to make the training logic more explicit.
Right now the science reads as plausible, but I want to see a clearer explanation of why each experimental aim maps to a specific gap in Allie's preparation as a future neurosurgeon-scientist.
The Specific Aims page also needs a sharper statement of what will be learned if the bivalent construct does not outperform the monovalent comparators, because reviewers may worry that the project is too dependent on Aim 1 working perfectly.
In the Research Strategy, the limitation of the orthotopic xenograft model should be stated more directly, especially the fact that immunodeficient mice cannot model the full human immune microenvironment.
Finally, the sponsor and co-sponsor roles are described well, but the collaborator roles could be tied more tightly to concrete deliverables so the mentoring team feels essential rather than decorative.
