## 3. Training, Activities and Timeline {#training-activities-and-timeline .unnumbered}

The training plan is designed to turn a motivated MD/PhD student into an early-stage neurosurgeon-scientist with practical expertise in GBM cellular immunotherapy.
The plan includes formal coursework, mentored research, structured meetings, scientific communication, and career development.

| Training need | Activity | Mentor | Timing | Product |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| CAR T-cell design | Weekly cell-therapy methods meetings and supervised construct review | Dr. Joseph-Louis Lagrange | FY0-FY1 | Finalized construct and assay plan |
| GBM biology | Neuro-oncology tumor board and brain tumor seminar | Dr. Emmy Noether | FY0-FY3 | Clinical context for model choices |
| Patient-derived models | Hands-on culture and orthotopic model training | Dr. Charles Hermite | FY0-FY2 | Validated model panel |
| Quantitative pathology | Image-analysis practicum and blinded residual-tumor quantification | Dr. Subrahmanyan Chandrasekhar | FY1-FY3 | Reproducible image-analysis workflow |
| Responsible translation | IND and early-phase trial design shadowing | Dr. Emmy Noether | FY2-FY3 | Draft translational roadmap |

### Mentored Research Meetings {.unnumbered}

I will meet weekly with Dr. Hermite during active experimental periods and at least twice monthly during analysis periods.
The full mentorship committee will meet quarterly to review milestones, troubleshoot barriers, and ensure that training activities remain aligned with my career goals.
Before each committee meeting, I will circulate a one-page progress summary with completed experiments, current obstacles, upcoming decisions, and manuscripts or abstracts in progress.

### Coursework and Workshops {.unnumbered}

I will complete advanced coursework or workshops in tumor immunology, biostatistics for preclinical studies, responsible conduct of research, and clinical trial design.
I will also attend institutional seminars in neuro-oncology, immunoengineering, and translational therapeutics.
When relevant, I will use short external workshops to fill focused gaps such as single-cell or spatial profiling interpretation.

### Career Development {.unnumbered}

The plan includes deliberate preparation for a physician-scientist career.
I will present annually at a national neuro-oncology or immunotherapy meeting.
I will submit at least one first-author manuscript from the F30 work and one review or methods manuscript related to antigen escape modeling.
I will meet with neurosurgeon-scientist role models to discuss residency selection, research continuity, and long-term funding strategy.
