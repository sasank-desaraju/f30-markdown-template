## 7. Training in the Responsible Conduct of Research (RCR) {#training-in-the-responsible-conduct-of-research-rcr .unnumbered}

The candidate will complete formal and ongoing instruction in the responsible conduct of research throughout the award period.
Training will include institutional RCR coursework, laboratory-based discussion, and project-specific mentoring.
Core topics will include data management, authorship, peer review, research misconduct, human-subjects protections, animal welfare, rigor and reproducibility, conflicts of interest, collaborative science, and responsible communication of preclinical findings.

The project raises specific ethical and translational issues.
Banked human tumor specimens must be handled with attention to consent, de-identification, privacy, and limits on re-identification.
Animal experiments must be justified by scientific necessity, designed with appropriate endpoints, and conducted with veterinary oversight.
Preclinical cellular-therapy data must be communicated without implying clinical benefit before safety and efficacy are established.

RCR topics will be revisited in quarterly mentorship meetings when they intersect with active project decisions.
Examples include authorship planning before manuscript drafting, data retention before sequencing analysis, blinding before image quantification, and humane endpoints before in vivo experiments.
This structure will make responsible conduct an active part of the research workflow rather than a one-time compliance activity.
