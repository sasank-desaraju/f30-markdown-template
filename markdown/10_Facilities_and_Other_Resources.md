# Facilities & Other Resources {.unnumbered}

## Overview

This synthetic application is set in a comprehensive academic health center with integrated resources in neurosurgery, neuro-oncology, immunology, cell engineering, animal modeling, flow cytometry, sequencing, and quantitative pathology.
The environment is designed to support an MD/PhD student who needs both rigorous laboratory training and clinically grounded mentorship.

## Neuro-Oncology Research Laboratory

The Hermite Laboratory maintains patient-derived glioblastoma culture workflows, tissue-processing protocols, and infrastructure for in vitro tumor-immunology assays.
The candidate will have access to biosafety cabinets, incubators, centrifuges, fluorescence microscopy, and shared laboratory space for co-culture experiments.

## Cell Engineering Core

The cell-engineering core supports vector design consultation, viral production coordination, T-cell activation workflows, and flow-based CAR expression assessment.
The core will provide training in safe handling of engineered cells and documentation practices for reproducible reagent generation.

## Brain Tumor Biorepository

The synthetic institutional biorepository contains de-identified standard-of-care surgical specimens and linked minimal metadata approved for research use.
This resource supports the project by providing patient-derived cultures with heterogeneous antigen expression.
No prospective therapeutic intervention is proposed in this F30.

## Flow Cytometry and Sequencing

Shared flow cytometry instrumentation will be used for tumor antigen profiling, CAR expression measurement, and T-cell phenotype analysis.
The genomics core will support RNA library preparation, sequencing, and standard quality-control review.
The candidate will perform downstream analysis with mentorship from the sponsor team and quantitative collaborators.

## Animal Modeling and Imaging

The animal facility supports orthotopic xenograft studies with veterinary oversight and approved procedures for humane endpoints.
Bioluminescence imaging resources are available for longitudinal tumor-burden assessment.
The quantitative pathology resource will support multiplex immunofluorescence imaging and residual-tumor analysis.

## Clinical Training Resources

The candidate will attend neuro-oncology tumor board, neurosurgery research conference, and translational therapeutics seminars.
These settings will help connect preclinical design decisions to clinical constraints, patient needs, and future trial development.

## MD/PhD Program Resources

The MD/PhD program provides career advising, physician-scientist seminars, grant-writing support, and peer mentorship.
These resources will support the candidate's transition from dissertation research back into clinical training while maintaining scientific momentum.
