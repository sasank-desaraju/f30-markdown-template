### Draft Collaborator Letter: Joseph-Louis Lagrange {.unnumbered}

Dear Review Committee,

I am pleased to support Allie Gator's proposed F30 training plan.
My role will be to provide consultation on CAR architecture, vector design, primary T-cell handling, and interpretation of potency and activation assays.
This support is most directly relevant to Aim 1, where Allie will engineer and benchmark a bivalent IL13Ra2/EGFRvIII CAR in primary human T cells.

Allie will have access to the synthetic Cell Engineering Core for construct-design consultation, transduction-workflow training, and assay-planning support.
I will meet with her monthly during construct design and Aim 1 execution and will be available for additional troubleshooting when needed.
We will pay particular attention to construct expression, donor-to-donor variability, cytokine-release interpretation, and the distinction between potency and excessive activation.

The proposed project is feasible for the F30 period because it pairs a focused biological question with established cell-engineering workflows.
Allie will gain practical training in the decisions that determine whether a CAR T-cell reagent is ready for rigorous preclinical testing.
I am enthusiastic about her potential and look forward to contributing to her development as a translational cellular-therapy investigator.

Sincerely,

Joseph-Louis Lagrange, PhD
