### Draft Collaborator Letter: Subrahmanyan Chandrasekhar {.unnumbered}

Dear Review Committee,

I am pleased to support Allie Gator's F30 application and her proposed training in quantitative pathology for glioblastoma cellular immunotherapy.
My group will provide mentorship in multiplex immunofluorescence design, residual-tumor image quantification, and reproducible analysis workflows.
This support is most directly relevant to Aim 3, where residual tumors from orthotopic patient-derived xenografts will be evaluated for antigen heterogeneity after monovalent or bivalent CAR T-cell pressure.

Allie will have access to our synthetic Quantitative Pathology Resource, including consultation on antibody panel design, imaging quality control, and blinded image-analysis plans.
I will meet with her monthly during Aim 3 preparation and execution.
My team will also provide focused troubleshooting sessions during assay optimization and image-analysis milestones.

This collaboration will help Allie connect biological escape hypotheses to spatial measurements in residual tumor tissue.
The training is well aligned with her goal of becoming a neurosurgeon-scientist who can evaluate cellular therapies using rigorous tissue-based endpoints.
I am enthusiastic about Allie's potential and about the value of this F30 training plan.

Sincerely,

Subrahmanyan Chandrasekhar, PhD
