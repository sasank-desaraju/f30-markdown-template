### Draft Co-Sponsor Letter: Emmy Noether {.unnumbered}

Dear Review Committee,

I am pleased to support Allie Gator's F30 application and to serve as co-sponsor.
Allie's project addresses a central challenge in recurrent glioblastoma therapy: early clinical responses to cellular therapy can occur, but durable control is limited by tumor heterogeneity and antigen escape.
The proposed work is significant because it frames antigen escape as a measurable biological phenotype and trains the candidate to evaluate cellular therapy with clinically informed preclinical models.

My role will be to ensure that Allie's research remains grounded in neuro-oncology practice and future translational feasibility.
I will mentor her in recurrent GBM clinical biology, tissue-access constraints, patient safety considerations, and early-phase trial logic.
Allie will attend neuro-oncology tumor board and relevant clinical research meetings so that her laboratory decisions are informed by the realities of patient care.
We will meet monthly and more often when project decisions require clinical or translational input.

Allie has the qualities needed for a successful physician-scientist career.
She asks clinically meaningful questions, learns quickly across disciplines, and responds well to rigorous feedback.
Her training gaps are appropriate for her career stage and are directly addressed by this application.
In particular, she will gain experience connecting preclinical endpoints, including tumor control and residual antigen heterogeneity, to future therapeutic hypotheses.

I am committed to supporting Allie's career development through the award period.
I will advise her on clinical re-entry planning, residency research continuity, presentation strategy, and long-term positioning as a neurosurgeon-scientist.
The proposed mentorship team is cohesive, complementary, and well suited to Allie's goals.

Sincerely,

Emmy Noether, MD
